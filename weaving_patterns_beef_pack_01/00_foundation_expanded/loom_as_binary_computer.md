# Loom As Binary Computer

A loom is a physical binary renderer. At every warp/weft intersection, one thread occupies the visible top position.

- warp over = `1`
- warp under = `0`

The drawdown is the image. The draft is the program. The loom is the machine. The cloth is the output.

## Why this matters

This lets weaving translate directly into shader logic, cellular automata, pixel grids, matrix art, jacquard-style image sampling, and generative pattern tools.

## Binary visual effects

A woven matrix produces:

- hard over-under contrast
- float length rhythm
- diagonal progression
- blocky figure-ground logic
- gridded repeat units
- directional light response
- structural limits that feel beautiful because they are real constraints
